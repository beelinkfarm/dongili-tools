How to Calculate Robot ROI

Calculating the return on investment for a robot requires comparing your current labor costs against the total cost of robot ownership. Start by determining your fully loaded labor cost: hourly wage multiplied by hours worked, adjusted for benefits (typically 1.3-1.4x base wage in the US).

Next, calculate the Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) for the robot. This goes beyond purchase price to include integration and installation (typically 20-80% of the robot price), annual maintenance (8-15%), software licensing, energy costs, and training. For humanoid robots, expect a TCO multiplier of 1.36-1.42x the purchase price over 3 years.

The ROI formula is: ROI% = ((Cumulative Labor Savings - Total Robot Investment) / Total Robot Investment) x 100. The payback period tells you how many months until the robot has saved more than it cost. Collaborative robots (cobots) typically achieve payback in 12-24 months, while humanoid robots may take 18-36 months depending on deployment scale.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you calculate robot ROI?

Robot ROI = ((Cumulative Labor Savings - Total Robot Investment) / Total Robot Investment) x 100%. Include purchase price, integration (1.36-1.42x multiplier for humanoids), annual maintenance, energy, and training costs versus displaced labor costs with benefits.

How much does a Tesla Optimus robot cost?

Tesla has stated a target price of $20,000-$30,000 for the Optimus humanoid robot. As of March 2026, Optimus is not yet commercially available; this is a 2027 target price, not a confirmed retail price.

What is the payback period for a cobot?

Collaborative robots like the Universal Robots UR series typically achieve payback in 12-24 months when replacing a single-shift worker, depending on labor costs and utilization rate.

How much does a Figure 02 robot cost?

Figure 02 does not have a confirmed purchase price. Figure AI offers the robot primarily through a Robot-as-a-Service (RaaS) model at approximately $1,000/month. Contact Figure AI for enterprise pricing.

What is robot Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)?

Robot TCO includes the purchase price plus integration/installation (20-80%), annual maintenance (8-15%), software licensing (5-15%), energy (2-5%), training, and end-of-life costs. Humanoid robots carry a 1.36-1.42x multiplier over 3 years.
